Research Areas

Exploring the intersection of mathematics, computation, and biology

Neural Excitability and Information Processing

Our lab investigates the biophysical mechanisms underlying neural excitability and information processing in the brain. We develop mathematical models of ion channel dynamics, synaptic transmission, and network activity to understand how neurons encode, process, and transmit information.

Current projects include:

  • Modeling adaptation and heterogeneity in neural responses
  • Investigating the role of ion channel stochasticity in neural coding
  • Characterizing the dynamics of spike and burst synchrony in neural networks
  • Understanding information processing in the visual system

Immune System Dynamics

We apply mathematical and computational approaches to understand the complex dynamics of the immune system. Our research focuses on modeling immune cell interactions, signaling pathways, and the dynamics of immune responses to pathogens and cancer.

Current projects include:

  • Mathematical modeling of T cell activation and differentiation
  • Stochastic modeling of immune cell migration and interaction
  • Understanding the dynamics of immune responses in autoimmune diseases
  • Investigating cellular mechanisms underlying inflammatory responses

Cellular Signaling and Gene Regulation

Our research explores the complex dynamics of cellular signaling pathways and gene regulatory networks. We develop mathematical models to understand how cells respond to external stimuli, maintain homeostasis, and regulate gene expression.

Current projects include:

  • Modeling calcium signaling dynamics in various cell types
  • Understanding the stochastic nature of gene expression
  • Investigating feedback mechanisms in signaling cascades
  • Exploring emergent properties of gene regulatory networks

Mathematical Physiology

We apply mathematical modeling to understand the complex dynamics of physiological systems. Our research covers a range of topics from cellular to organ-level processes, with a focus on how these systems maintain function and respond to perturbations.

Current projects include:

  • Mathematical modeling of hormonal regulation and endocrine systems
  • Understanding the dynamics of insulin secretion and glucose regulation
  • Modeling cardiovascular system dynamics
  • Investigating mechanisms of homeostasis in physiological systems

Research Methods

Our approach to complex biological problems

Mathematical Modeling

We develop mathematical models using differential equations, stochastic processes, and network theory to describe biological systems at various scales.

Computational Simulation

We implement our models in computational environments like MATLAB and Python to simulate system behavior under various conditions.

Data Analysis

We analyze experimental data using statistical methods and custom algorithms to extract meaningful patterns and parameters.

Machine Learning

We apply machine learning techniques to classify biological data, predict system behavior, and optimize model parameters.

Our Research Workflow

1

Identify Biological Question

We work with experimental collaborators to identify important biological questions that can benefit from mathematical approaches.

2

Develop Mathematical Model

We formulate mathematical representations of the biological system, incorporating key components and interactions.

3

Computational Implementation

We implement the model computationally and perform simulations to explore system behavior.

4

Validate with Experimental Data

We compare model predictions with experimental data to validate our approach and refine the model.

5

Generate New Insights and Hypotheses

We use the validated model to generate new insights and testable hypotheses about the biological system.
